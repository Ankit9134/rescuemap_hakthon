Cyclone Precautions and Safety: Be Prepared, Be Aware

Before a Cyclone:

  • Pay attention to weather reports and warnings issued by official sources. Sign up for emergency alerts in your area.
  • Prepare your home: Trim trees and remove loose objects from around your house that could become projectiles in high winds. Board up windows with plywood or secure them with storm shutters.
  • Assemble an emergency kit: This should include non-perishable food, water, a first-aid kit, medications, flashlights, batteries, a battery-powered radio, sanitation supplies, and important documents.

During a Cyclone:

  • Evacuate if instructed: If you are ordered to evacuate, do so immediately. Don't delay and risk getting trapped by flooding or debris.
  • Take shelter: If you cannot evacuate, go to a designated storm shelter or the most interior room in your house, preferably a basement, away from windows and doors.
  • Stay informed: Continue to listen to weather updates on a battery-powered radio for the latest information and instructions.

After a Cyclone:

  • Stay indoors: Wait for an official "all-clear" before going outside.
  • Beware of hazards: Watch out for downed power lines, damaged buildings, and flooding. Do not enter floodwaters which may be contaminated or hiding debris.
  • Check on neighbors: Help those who may be injured or need assistance.
  • Report damage: Contact your local authorities to report any damage to your property.
