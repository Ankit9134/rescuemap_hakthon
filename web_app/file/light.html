Linghting Precautions and Safety: Be Prepared, Be Aware

When Outdoors:

  • Shelter Immediately: If you hear thunder or see lightning, find safe shelter promptly. The best options are substantial buildings or enclosed, metal-roofed vehicles with the windows rolled up. Avoid open structures like gazebos or bus stops.
  • Stay Away from Tall Objects: Don't stand under tall trees, telephone poles, or other elevated objects that lightning is more likely to strike.
  • Avoid Open Areas: Stay away from open spaces like parks, beaches, golf courses, and swimming pools.
  • Don't Lie Down: Don't lie flat on the ground. If you must take cover outdoors, crouch low on the balls of your feet with your hands tucked between your knees.
  • Water: Stay away from bodies of water, including lakes, rivers, and pools. Lightning can travel through water.
  • Discounted Golf: Contrary to the myth, avoid using golf clubs during a storm. They are tall conductors and can attract lightning strikes.

    • Indoors:

    • Stay Off Corded Electronics: Avoid using corded phones, computers, or appliances during a thunderstorm. Lightning can travel through electrical wiring.
    • Avoid Plumbing: Don't take a shower, bath, wash dishes, or do laundry during a storm. Plumbing can conduct electricity.
    • Stay Away from Windows and Doors: Lightning can strike near windows and doors. Avoid leaning on them or standing close by.

    General Safety Tips:

    • Monitor the Weather: Stay informed about weather forecasts and warnings for thunderstorms.
    • Postpone Outdoor Activities: If possible, reschedule outdoor activities for days with no thunderstorms predicted.
    • Unplug Electronics: Consider unplugging electronics from power outlets if there's a high risk of lightning strikes in your area.
    • Surge Protectors: Using surge protectors for valuable electronics can provide some additional protection from lightning strikes.
