Before a Flood:

  • Informed: Monitor weather reports and flood warnings issued by official sources. Sign up for emergency alerts in your area.
  • Know Your Flood Risk: Identify if your property is in a flood plain. Research historical flood levels in your area.
  • Flood Proofing: Consider measures like elevating utilities, installing flood barriers, or sealing crawl spaces (if applicable) to minimize flood damage.
  • Prepare Your Home: Secure loose objects outside that could become projectiles in floodwaters. Move important belongings to higher levels.

During a Flood:

  • Evacuate if Instructed: Don't hesitate if authorities order an evacuation. Don't risk getting trapped by floodwaters.
  • Move to Higher Ground: If evacuation is not possible, move to the highest level of your home and stay away from windows and doors.
  • Turn Off Utilities: If safe to do so, turn off electricity and gas at the main valves to prevent fires and hazards.
  • Stay Informed: Use a battery-powered radio for the latest weather updates and instructions from emergency services.

After a Flood:

  • Stay Indoors: Wait for an official "all-clear" before venturing outside. Floodwaters may be contaminated or hiding debris.
  • Beware of Hazards: Watch out for downed power lines, damaged buildings, and contaminated water.
  • Inspect Your Home: Be cautious when entering your home. Look for structural damage, electrical hazards, and gas leaks.
  • Document Damage: Take photos and videos of flood damage to your property for insurance purposes.
