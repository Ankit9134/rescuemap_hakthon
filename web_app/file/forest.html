Forest Fire Precautions and Safety: Be Prepared, Be Aware

Before a Forest Fire:

  • Informed: Closely monitor fire danger ratings and weather forecasts issued by official sources. Sign up for emergency alerts in your area.
  • Create a Defensible Space: Clear flammable vegetation like leaves, dead branches, and debris around your home for at least 30 feet (10 meters). This creates a barrier that can slow the spread of fire.
  • Prepare an Emergency Kit: Assemble a kit with non-perishable food, water, first-aid supplies, medications, flashlights, batteries, a battery-powered radio, sanitation supplies, and important documents. Consider including a respirator or mask for smoke inhalation.
  • Develop an Evacuation Plan: Discuss escape routes with your family and practice them regularly. Identify a designated meeting place outside the fire zone.

During a Forest Fire:

  • if Instructed: Don't hesitate if authorities order an evacuation. Don't risk getting trapped by flames or smoke. Follow designated evacuation routes and avoid congested areas.
  • Follow Instructions: Pay close attention to instructions from emergency personnel. They will provide updates on the fire situation and evacuation procedures.
  • If Trapped Indoors: Close all windows and doors to keep smoke out. Seal cracks around doors and windows with wet towels or cloths. Shelter in a room on the interior of the house, preferably a basement. Stay low to the ground and cover your mouth and nose with a damp cloth.

After a Forest Fire:

  • Stay Away from the Area: Wait for official clearance before returning to the fire zone. Areas may still be smoldering and pose dangers like falling trees or unstable structures.
  • Report Damage: Contact local authorities to report any damage to your property.
  • Be Aware of Air Quality: Smoke and ash from wildfires can irritate your lungs. Wear a mask if you are outdoors and experiencing respiratory discomfort.

Additional Tips:

  • Campfire Safety: If you have a campfire, build it in a designated fire pit and extinguish it completely before leaving the area. Drown the fire with water and stir the ashes to ensure no embers remain.
  • Cigarette Disposal: Never discard cigarettes on dry ground or from a moving vehicle. Extinguish cigarettes properly in a designated ashtray.
  • Spark Aware: Avoid using machinery or equipment that could generate sparks in dry, flammable areas.
