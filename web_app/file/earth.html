Earthquake Precautions and Safety: Be Prepared, Be Aware

Before an Earthquake:

  • Assemble an Emergency Kit: Prepare a backpack or designated area with essential supplies like water, non-perishable food, first-aid kit, flashlight, batteries, radio, and important documents.
  • Earthquake Plan: Discuss and practice an earthquake plan with your family. Decide on meeting locations after an earthquake and designate a safe spot indoors (Drop, Cover, Hold on) and outdoors (open area away from buildings).
  • Hazard Mitigation: Secure furniture, cabinets, and shelves to walls to prevent them from falling during tremors. Identify and fix potential hazards around your house like loose wires or heavy picture frames.

During an Earthquake:

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On: If indoors, immediately drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy table or desk, and hold on until the shaking stops. Avoid windows, doors, and hanging objects.
  • Stay Indoors (if possible): If you're indoors during an earthquake, the safest place is generally under a sturdy piece of furniture. Only evacuate if there's a fire or immediate danger of building collapse.
  • Outdoors: If outdoors, move to a clear open area away from buildings, trees, power lines, and other hazards.

After an Earthquake:

  • Check for Injuries: Check yourself and others for injuries and provide first aid if needed.
  • Expect Aftershocks: Be prepared for aftershocks, smaller earthquakes that can follow the main one.
  • Listen to Authorities: Stay informed by listening to the radio or following instructions from local authorities.
  • Check for Damage: Carefully inspect your surroundings for structural damage, gas leaks, or fire hazards.
  • Evacuate if Necessary: If authorities recommend evacuation, follow their instructions and proceed calmly to the designated areas.
