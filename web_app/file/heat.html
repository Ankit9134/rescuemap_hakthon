Heat Wave Precautions and Safety: Be Prepared, Be Aware

Stay Hydrated:

  • plenty of water throughout the day, even if you don't feel thirsty. Aim for cool water or rehydration drinks like buttermilk (lassi) or homemade lemonade (nimbu pani).
  • Avoid sugary drinks, excessive caffeine, and alcohol, as they dehydrate you.
  • a water bottle with you whenever you go outside.

Beat the Heat:

  • outdoor activity: Avoid strenuous exercise or work during the hottest part of the day, typically between 12 noon and 3 PM.
  • Seek air conditioning or cooling shelters: If possible, spend time in air-conditioned spaces like libraries, malls, or designated cooling centers.
  • Take cool showers or baths: This can help lower your body temperature quickly.

Prepare Your Home:

  • windows and doors closed during the hottest part of the day. Open them at night for cooler air circulation.
  • Use curtains, blinds, or sunshades on windows facing the sun. This helps block heat from entering your home.
  • If you don't have air conditioning, use fans and traditional cooling methods like keeping wet mats or cloths on doors and windows.

Look Out for Others:

  • Never leave children or pets unattended in parked vehicles. Even with windows cracked open, the temperature inside a car can rise rapidly to dangerous levels.
  • Check on vulnerable individuals: Especially elderly people living alone who may be more susceptible to heatstroke. Offer them water or help them stay cool.
