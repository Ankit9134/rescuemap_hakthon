Fire Precautions and Safety: Be Prepared, Be Aware

Prevention:

  • Smoke Alarms: Install smoke alarms on every level of your home, inside and outside sleeping areas. Test them monthly and replace batteries regularly.
  • Cooking Safety: Stay focused while cooking. Keep flammable objects away from the stovetop and oven. Clean cooking surfaces regularly to avoid grease buildup.
  • Electrical Safety: Avoid overloading outlets. Don't run electrical cords under rugs or furniture. Have a qualified electrician fix any damaged wiring.
  • Heating Sources: Maintain heating appliances and have them serviced regularly. Keep space heaters away from furniture and flammable materials.

Escape Planning:

  • Plan and Practice: Develop a fire escape plan with your family and practice it twice a year. Discuss two escape routes from each room in case one is blocked.
  • Clear Exits: Ensure all exits are clear and easy to open from the inside. Don't block exits with furniture or clutter.
  • Meeting Place: Designate a meeting place outside your home where everyone will gather after escaping a fire.

In Case of Fire:

  • GET OUT. STAY OUT. CALL FOR HELP: This is the most important rule. Evacuate immediately when the fire alarm sounds or you smell smoke. Don't waste time trying to save belongings.
  • Feel the Door: Before opening a door, feel it with the back of your hand. If it's hot, don't open it. Use your alternative escape route.
  • >Stay Low: Smoke and heat rise. Crawl low to the ground to avoid breathing in smoke.
  • Call 911 (or your local emergency number): Once you are safely outside, call emergency services from a neighbor's phone or a cell phone.
