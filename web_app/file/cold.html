Cold Precautions and Safety: Be Prepared, Be Aware

Before a Cold Wave:

  • Informed: Closely monitor weather reports and warnings issued by official sources.
  • Dress in Layers: Prepare warm clothes, including thermals, gloves, hats, and scarves. Wear loose-fitting layers that trap heat more effectively than a single bulky garment.
  • Stock Up on Supplies: Get enough non-perishable food, water, and medications to last for several days in case of power outages or limited mobility.
  • Prepare Your Home: Seal air leaks around windows and doors to prevent drafts. Insulate exposed pipes to prevent freezing. Ensure your heating system is functioning properly.

During a Cold Wave:

  • Stay Indoors: Limit your time outdoors, especially for vulnerable individuals like children and the elderly.
  • Stay Warm and Dry: Wear warm clothing and layers, and keep a dry change of clothes handy in case you get wet.
  • Heat Your Home Safely: Use space heaters cautiously and keep them away from flammable materials. Ensure proper ventilation to avoid carbon monoxide poisoning.
  • Monitor for Signs of Hypothermia: Shivering, confusion, slurred speech, and drowsiness are all potential signs. If you suspect hypothermia, seek medical attention immediately.

Additional Tips:

  • Pet Safety: Bring outdoor pets indoors during extreme cold or provide them with a well-insulated shelter.
  • Care: Ensure your car is winterized with antifreeze and proper tire pressure. Keep a winter emergency kit in your car in case you get stranded.
  • Power Outages: Have a battery-powered radio and flashlights on hand for emergencies.
